## position
- Given a [[Reference Frame|reference frame]], the position of a point can be specified by giving the radius vector $\vec{x}$ which goes from the origin of the frame to the point.
- In a [[3-D Coordinate System|Cartesian frame]], the components of $\hat{x}$ with respect to the three axes $X_{1}, X_{2}, X_{3}$ of the frame, are the coordinates $x_{1}, x_{2}, x_{3}$. We can write $$x=\sum_{i=1}^{3} x_{i}\vec{e}_{i}\equiv x_{1}\vec{e}_{1}+x_{2}\vec{e}_{2}+x_{3}\vec{e}_{3}$$ where $\vec{e}_{i}$ is the unit vector in the $i^{th}$ direction.
## distance
- The definition of distance is known as the [[Metric|metric]] of the space for which it is being defined.
- The Euclidean metric defines the distance $D$ between two points $x$ and $y$, with the coordinates $x_{i}$ and $y_{i}$, as $$D =\sqrt{\sum_{i=1} (x_{i}-y_{i})^2 }$$
