# Parametric Equation
- There are great many curves that canâ€™t be written as in terms of only $x,y$ to deal with this we introduce **parametric equations**.
- Instead of defining $y$ in terms of $x$ or vice-versa we define both $x$ and $y$ in terms of a third variable called the **parameter** as follows,$$x=f(t)\hspace{0.5in} y=g(t)$$
# Common Parametric Curves
| Curve                                                            | Parametric Equations                                                                                                 |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\dfrac{x^{2}}{a^{2}}+\dfrac{y^{2}}{b^{2}}=1$ (Ellipse)          | $x=acos(t),\hspace{0.15in} y=b\sin(y),\hspace{0.15in} 0\leq t\leq 2\pi$                                              |
| $x^{2}+y^{2}=r^{2}$(Circle)                                      | $x=r\cos(t),\hspace{0.15in}y-r\cos(t),\hspace{0.15in}0\leq t\leq 2\pi$                                               |
| Line Segment from $(x_{0},y_{0},z_{0})$ to $(x_{1},y_{1},z_{1})$ | $\vec{r}(t)=(1-t)\langle x_{0},y_{0},z_{0}\rangle+t \langle x_{1},y_{1},z_{1} \rangle,\hspace{0.25in}0 \leq t\leq 1$ |
